  • Do Project Euler Problems!

    • These are challenging, and very stimulating math problems that are too big to solve by hand. But you can solve them with code! I recommend first writing out your ideas on paper and writing your algorithms/functions in English first so even if the syntax of Javascript is getting in the way, you're able to show the ideas behind your code even if your code isn't where you want it to be.

    2. Download Twine (the tool we've been using to make interactive stories) from the software center on your computer. You can also download it from Twine's website.

    3. Layout your story in Twine: Watch this tutorial..

    4. Put together the pages/blocks of your story and conect them via [[links]]. Here's what it will look like:

    Make Arrow-click Childrens Book (like these)

    1. Make a blueprint: Make a storyboard for your story


    2. Download the framework: Download reveal.js pressing clone or download zip" on the right hand corner of the github page and choosing "download zip".


    3. Open the code: Open the "index.html" file in sublime text by either dragging it into an open sublime text document OR by pressing on it with two fingers and choosing "open with >> sublime text".


    4. Add and style your text: each [section][/section] tag (haha, I can't type the "<'s" because this website is made of code) is one slide. To add text, I'd add [p class="fragment" ][/p] (paragraph tags with the class of fragment, around your text, within each section. I'd also add a google font that you like by adding the font link to the top of your HTML page in the "head" section and by creating a "style" section in the head and setting .fragment{ font-family:____" }


    5. Make your images: in paper! or something else you like! Use photography from your phone — macrophotography looks especially good!


    6. Add your images: Inn each slide/section, add a data-background-image="" attribute:
      [section data-background-image="imgs/yourImage.png"]
